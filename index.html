Becoming a judge in 2015 was the pinnacle of my legal career. I enjoyed serving as a Judge because I felt that I was using my legal experience and other skills to help lawyers and everyday people reach timely conclusions to their legal disputes. During trials and on docket day I was able to have genuine conversations about the law and facts in many different types of cases. While straight ticket voting cut my judicial service short, I do not believe it is over, which is why I am running to return to the bench that I held, the 61st District Court.

My judicial philosophy is simple: I believe that a Texas Trial Court Judge should show up to work on time and work full days, allow all parties to be timely heard in person or by phone, read all submissions before hearings, timely rule on all motions, treat lawyers, jurors and all participants equally and with respect, serve the people with integrity and follow the rule of law. I abided by these principles when I served in 2015-2016 and will do so again.
